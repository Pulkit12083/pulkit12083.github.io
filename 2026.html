Thank you by Dido is such a beautiful song. I generally don't like pop music because of its formulaic and cliche melodies, things done to death, and because of my own personal tendency to be interested in and lured more by things that are weird, different, niche. But there's something about this particular song that I find just really nice. It's comforting to me and i don't mind publicly professing my feelings of love for it.

Her voice is sweet. and she sings about how comforting and helpful it is for her to be around the person she's singing about. her life is difficult and the weather is grey, but it's okay because she has this person's picture on her wall. she's running late for work and all the coworkers might imply that she'd get fired soon, but then she gets a call from the person she's singing about and it all feels not that bad after all. She then sings a "thank you" for them for giving her the best day of her life. It's so sweet. And I think I find it so because I can somewhat relate to it ~ having known someone who I miss and who made a lot of my days meaningful and precious even though they would have otherwise been bad. There are times when I want to sing this song out of love and care for her (the person in my life, not Dido (or maybe even Dido, for writing and singing this song and thereby helping me recognize this feeling and express it)).

The song is comforting to me in other ways too - the melodious flute and gentle guitar strumming. And it has the general soft musical aesthetic of a relaxed 90s song that i find very comforting. I don't remember how i discovered it but it has grown on me since i did.

There's something interesting about its music video however. when I first saw it, I was a little perplexed. It shows Dido in a nice pink colored house, looking into the camera calmly and singing, while there are stern-faced officials who have come to evict her, and who then barge into the house to repossess all of her possessions inside it! All while she's singing about this person who has helped add meaning and goodness to her life! She's really calm through it all too - looking into the camera with an expression that i cannot tell if is a little sad or a little grateful. Maybe it is both. Maybe it is better for this to remain ambiguous. It is a little bit confusing however to see her remain so composed and sing about a loved one while getting removed from her beautiful home. Is this okay? Is this a form of love that's reasonable and healthy? When I was in a relationship, I always thought about how it was important that it be a positive force in our lives and to not forget that other aspects of life (outside the relationship) were important and meaningful too. It felt important to remember to take care of ourselves (by our I mean me and my partner). It would be a little melancholic to see love blind us and inadvertently become to our detriment, coloring the relationship in shades that are gray and sad - introducing mixed feelings to something that felt so vibrant, beautiful, and life-affirming. I wonder if that's how Dido feels about her life as her beautiful house is getting taken apart from around her, as the flowers in her garden get trampled by boots and a bulldozer, and she's forcibly taken out to the street. The closest thing she sings about the situation occurring in the video is:

"And even if my house falls down now,
I wouldn't have a clue,
Because you're near me.."

Wouldn't it be really sad and tragic to see one's house and everything in it fall down? And not be able to even recognize it from happening because one is so taken by someone else.. Or is it a very touching and beautiful thing? - to be part of a love so affirming and nice that the travesties in life don't even register or matter that much. I don't know. When I first saw the video i wondered if it was secretly a song about the tragicness of the blinding ability of love and how it can bring about one's downfall - and I thought this while I was actually doing well in life and in a relationship! But there are days when I think that Dido sang this song very sincerely and meant it without any irony and the lyrics are very heartfelt - and I think this while I am doing terrible in life, having had a downfall, and am not in a relationship anymore! Hah. The tipsy-turvy nature of things. It would make more sense if my perception of the video was more aligned by my personal circumstances at the time of seeing it, but they're somehow the exact opposite lol.

The next few lyrics in the song are:

"And I want to thank you
For giving me the best day of my life
Oh, just to be with you,
is having the best day of my life"

and as she sings, I think I truly understand what she means because I've felt this way about someone too and genuinely feel so grateful and glad that i encountered her and had some of the best days of my life because of her being in them. While Dido sings this part, it shows her house getting bulldozed and completely destroyed! It seems like that Dido in the video is likely having the worst day of her life! Is she singing sincerely or ironically??

After everything that has happened in my life - with the beautiful and meaningful relationship that comes to my mind on hearing Dido in the song and the way in which it ended being so painful that it destroyed me in many ways that comes to my mind on seeing Dido in the video, I've come to lean more towards the theory that she's singing sincerely. That even as her house has been torn down, she feels grateful and glad for her partner's presence in her life. The partner .. that is nowhere near her in the video as something really unfortunate is happening in her life.

Now wait a minute. All this while I've been talking about this person being her partner. This is what I assumed about the person being referred to by her all this while. But it could just as well be a friend, or a parent, or one's child even. It still seems most fitting for the song to be about a partner, but there's some ambiguity here too. As I was seeing the music video while writing about it just now, it dawned on me that the entire time she is looking into the camera, at ME! IS the person she's referring to the whole time .. me? That can't be it - She does't know me at all and I've not been around her. Maybe it is about the people who listen to her music. But then what about the references to the picture on her wall, her getting the call on the way to work, and her getting handed the towel as she returns from outside soaking wet due to the rain? It has to be someone more personal than me or the general person who listens to her music. So I guess we're back to a lover or a partner being the most likely person in question here.

Are all these little details in the video deliberate? The partner not being around her as her house is torn down, her looking into the camera (at us) while singing thank you, her not revealing her emotions very clearly as everything is happening. oh the ambiguity and bittersweetness of love..
